<p><p>The heat-transfer fluid market is primarily categorized into synthetic organic heat transfer fluids and mineral oils. Synthetic organic fluids are engineered for high thermal stability and efficiency, often used in demanding industrial applications. They tend to have a wider operational temperature range and lower viscosity, enhancing system performance. In contrast, mineral oils are derived from petroleum and are widely used due to their cost-effectiveness and availability. They operate effectively in moderate temperature settings but may degrade at higher temperatures, limiting their application range.</p></p>
