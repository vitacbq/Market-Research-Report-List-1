<p><p>The cavity oscillator market is segmented into two main types: microwave chambers and radio frequency (RF) cavities. Microwave chambers are utilized for generating microwave frequencies, primarily in applications such as communications and radar systems, offering high precision and stability. In contrast, RF cavities are designed for generating radio frequencies, commonly used in particle accelerators and various industrial processes. Both types play crucial roles in modern technology, catering to specific needs across telecommunications, scientific research, and various engineering applications.</p></p>
